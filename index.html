All About Chess!



A Brief History of Chess

Chess is the world's most popular and recognized games, and originated around 1500 years ago in India. It quickly become popular, and spread throughout the ancient world. As time went on, the rules changed and the game evolved. The modern chess that we all play today originates from Europe, and is slightly different from the original chess in India. This website will teach you how to play the modern chess we all know of today, as well as hints, tips, and tricks to make you a good player.



How Do You Play Chess?

So you may be wondering. Even if I know or just learned all the rules of Chess, how exactly do I access the game in order to play it? Well, Chess is usually played physically with a board and physical pieces, however you don't exactly need a physical set of chess to play chess. We have the internet today, which of course, contains online chess. Many websites host chess avaliable for you to play online against other real people or bots, allowing you to practice and hone your skills. You can play competitively against other players from around the world, or play casually with your friends.

Physical Chess

Physical chess is the most traditional way to play the game. Originally, they were made out of expensive materials such as wood, ivory, or other precious materials, but today, you can easily buy a cheap plastic set of chess. Although each set may vary in looks, the rules are always the exact same, and the board will always have the same traits such as the 8x8 grid, 2 colors, and the same pieces, just maybe in a different style.

Competitive Chess

Although chess is a great casual game for relaxation, competitive chess is on a whole differenet level. It requires high skill and intense, strategic thinking. The opponents are usually very skilled, and the stakes are much higher. Some chess tournaments offer real money prizes and the opportunity to compete at even higher levels, such as internationally. The game is so intense, that players often sweat a lot or even fold under a lot of pressure. Some say it is more stressfull than an actual physical sport!

Skill Level

In competitive chess, a player's skill is often measured by their rating or ELO score, which is a numerical representation of their skill level. The higher the better, as you gain when you win and lose when you lose. You can see examples of ELO ratings on many online chess websites such as chess.com, where a lot of top players reside, however it is important to note that just because someone doesn't have a high elo rating on online websites such as chess.com shouldn't mean they aren't skilled players. Remember, chess tournaments are often played physically to prevent cheating, such as using an AI bot like Stockfish to calculate the best moves and win, but that doesn't stop insanely determined cheaters who do the craziest things to cheat, such as using morse code vibrations in their shoes.







Chess is a great casual game that requires some thinking and stratagy, but competitive chess is on a completley different level!
