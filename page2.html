How to Play Standard Chess

Chess seems complex at first glance, but its actually pretty simple.

There are 2 sides in chess. White and Black. White always has the first move, no matter what.

Chess is a game played on an 8x8 grid, with 16 pieces for each player. Each piece has a different way of moving, and some even have their own unique mechanics. The goal of the game is to checkmate your opponent's king, essentially trapping it on a position where it cannot move anywhere.

However it is important to understand that checkmate is not the only way to end the game. You can also Stalemate, or tie the game.

If the players reach an agreement, or simply decide to agree to a draw and stop playing, then the game is ended as a Stalemate.

If there are not enough pieces on the board to force a checkmate, cases where it is literally impossible to force a checkmate, then the game declared as a Stalemate.

If a player repeats the same moves 3 times in a row, for example moving a piece back and fourth repeatidly, then the game is also declared as a stalemate.

If the game has dragged on for 50 consecutive moves where neither a player has moved a pawn or captured a piece, usually where all the pieces are taken, then the game is also declared as a Stalemate.

How to Set up The Board

For more information on piece identification and their moves, click here.

The board is always set up the same every game, and will always remain the same.

The correct setup involves placing the board so that each player has a white square on their right side.

The pawns are always put in the 2nd row from the edge, in a wall, essentially forming a wall.

Then the Rooks go on the bottom left and right corners, below the pawns.

The two knights are then placed right next to the rooks.

The bishops are then placed next to the knights, each in a different colored square.

Then the queen and king are placed in the last 2 remaining slots, with each queen of their color on their corresponding color square. So for example, a white queen will be placed on the inner white square, while the black one is placed on the inner black square.
